Vietnam

Located in Southeast Asia, Vietnam is occupied by almost 100 million people and covers the east side of Southeast Asia with its neighboring countries being
being Laos, Cambodia, Thailand, and China.


Vietnam is widely known for its clean, lovely beaches and clear waters. They're also commonly known for having houses and other buildings laying on their waters.
In Vietnamese culture, it is common to do Water Puppetry. Water Puppetry is is an old, unique art form that contains storytelling, music, and puppetry performed
on water They also have the stunning landscapes. One being the Hao Long Bay which have limestone mountains that rise up emerald waters.

Hanoi

Hanoi is one of Vietnam's Capital, Saigon was once one. This was because of the French colonial period in 1954 caused Vietnam splitting into two where Saigon was
democratic in the South while Hanoi was communist in the North. In the year 1976 and till this day, Vietnam has refused independence for Vietnam, but eventually
eventually were forced leave because of the Vietnamese War and the Vietnamese people rebelling against them.

Historical Sites

  • Hoa Lo Prison

    • At Hoa Lo Prison, you'll have the experience of learning about prisoners treatment. Its significance in the Vietnamese War is to give listeners a small taste
    of what the American soldiers endured during this time period. Many officers who served are seen as Vietnamese War heroes till this day.

  • Temple of Literature

    • Built in 1070, Emperor Ly Thanh Tong built the templt to honor Confucius and his disciples and Vietnam's first official university. People would study Confucian
    teachings and take civil examinations. Many upper-class and famous people often came to this school so they may prosper in the teachings of their country and ancestors.

  • Hanoi Opera House

    • A cultural center that hosts not only performances, but educational events and social gatherings. This is popular to many tourists because cultural
    entertainment performances are often hosted here. Things such as bamboo criques, contemporary dance, orchestra, concerts, and government events.
